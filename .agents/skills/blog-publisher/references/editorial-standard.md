# Editorial standard

Use this before drafting and before approving any public blog item.

## The bar

A publishable item must be more than safe and syntactically valid. It should give a stranger a memorable judgment, a usable model, or a concrete decision aid.

## Hard blockers

Do not publish if any are true:

- The piece is a cleaned-up internal note with no public thesis.
- The reader payoff is vague: the reader cannot say what decision, model, or warning they gained.
- It mostly restates source material without synthesis, contrast, examples, or boundaries.
- It exposes secrets, internal paths in public prose, private markers, private personal/company data, or forbidden-publish material.
- It fabricates shipped results, public demos, customer feedback, external endorsement, or personal experience not supported by source evidence.
- It makes fast-moving vendor/model/protocol/benchmark/security claims without current official/primary-source refresh or explicit uncertainty.
- Security content includes operational attack instructions beyond defensive framing.

## Quality rubric: 18 points

Minimum to sync: 14/18. Minimum to publish/deploy: 16/18 and no blocker.

| item | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thesis | no clear point | topic is clear but claim is soft | sharp, memorable central claim |
| Reader payoff | internal record | some useful facts | clear decision/model/action for reader |
| Specificity | generic | has some examples | concrete scenario, contrast, or failure mode |
| Structure | notes dump | readable but loose | purposeful arc with clean transitions |
| Source grounding | unclear | source listed | key claims trace to source/refreshed evidence |
| Judgment density | summary only | occasional judgment | tradeoffs, boundaries, and opinions are explicit |
| Voice | sterile or mentor-note tone | acceptable | public, concise, human, not content-farm |
| Safety/privacy | unresolved risk | mostly safe | no leak/fabrication/private-data risk |
| Freshness | stale/unstated | partially refreshed | current enough or uncertainty clearly scoped |

## Required editorial questions

Answer these in the review file:

1. What one sentence should the reader remember?
2. What reader decision does this help?
3. What is the strongest counterpoint or edge case?
4. What did we remove from the source because it was private, weak, or too internal?
5. What would make this piece not worth publishing yet?

## Shape expectations

- Essays should have a thesis, context, concrete examples, tradeoffs, and ending judgment.
- Notes should be one strong idea, not a small essay pretending to be a note.
- Links should say why this source matters now, not just summarize the source.
- Logs should preserve time context and learning movement; avoid fake certainty.
- Projects should show role, problem, constraints, process evidence, and current status; do not inflate results.
