Design note: a personal site should reveal the operating system, not just the output.

A portfolio shows finished artifacts. A blog shows posts. The interesting middle ground is a living manual: the questions being investigated, the systems being built, and the taste that decides what is worth publishing.

The visual system can feel like a lab, but the writing still has to feel like a person thinking clearly.

That means every piece of Markdown — a project note, a log entry, or an essay — should survive without special decoration. The interface can provide atmosphere, but the content must carry the signal.
